I enrolled at UTD as a premed student, and I believe the on campus opportunities and academics set me up very well for medical school. I was able to engage in basic science research for all four years, serve as a teaching assistant, work as a tutor at the student success center, and engage in activities such as the on campus EMS agency. The advising committee is helpful in preparing students for the medical school application process (compiling letters of recommendation, reviewing applications, and offering mock interviews). They are generous with financial aid and take promoting equity in higher education seriously.

UTD strongly supports activities match as chess and e-sports and there are options to explore the arts as well (acapella groups, theatre, choir, orchestra, etc.). If there were one thing I would like to see change in the future, it would be to place more of an emphasis on atheletics because it is a quintessential college experience that UTD students seem to miss out on.

I went to UTD for 4 years and I unfortunately had a very bad experience. I transferred to another school because I didn't feel safe there anymore due to the escalating political climate (protests, arrests, etc). Especially as a woman, it's not a safe environment. I knew several girls who were assaulted, harassed, or stalked (or worse) on or near campus. I went there for engineering, which it's known for, but after transferring to another school I realized that many of the professors at UTD are not good lecturers or you simply cannot understand them because of how strong their accents are. The advisors aren't helpful and can be discouraging. UTD advertises itself as "diverse", and I suppose it is more diverse than schools that are 80% white US citizens, but when you spend time on campus you realize it's not really that diverse and if you're not from the same background as the students then it's hard to make friendships in class. I think they get good ratings because of research.

As a freshman at the University of Texas at Dallas, my experience has been both exciting and challenging. The campus is modern and well-maintained, with plenty of study spaces, libraries, and recreational areas. Professors are knowledgeable and approachable, and I’ve found that attending office hours really helps deepen my understanding of course material. The class sizes are manageable, which makes it easier to ask questions and participate in discussions. I also appreciate the variety of student organizations and events, which provide opportunities to meet new people and explore interests outside of academics. Navigating campus life and balancing coursework has been a learning experience, but overall, I feel supported and motivated to succeed. UTD has given me a strong foundation as I begin my college journey.
